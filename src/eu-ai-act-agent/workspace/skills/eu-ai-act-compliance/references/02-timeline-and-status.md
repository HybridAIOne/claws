# Timeline & Current Status

*Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. Compiled 22 June 2026. Not legal advice.*
**Read this file before quoting any application date.** The headline dates are
in flux because of the Digital Omnibus (see §3).

## 1. Core identity (enacted)

- **Full title:** Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 of the European Parliament and of the
  Council of 13 June 2024 laying down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence
  (Artificial Intelligence Act).
- **Adopted:** 13 June 2024. **Published in the Official Journal:** 12 July 2024.
- **Entry into force:** **1 August 2024** (20th day after publication, Art. 113).
- Structure: a **risk-based** law — prohibited (unacceptable) risk, high risk,
  limited risk (transparency), minimal risk — plus a separate track for
  general-purpose AI (GPAI) models.

## 2. Phased application dates — as enacted in Article 113

| Milestone | Date | What starts applying |
| --- | --- | --- |
| +6 months | **2 February 2025** | **Prohibited practices (Art. 5)** and **AI literacy (Art. 4)**. *(In force now.)* |
| +12 months | **2 August 2025** | **GPAI model obligations (Ch. V)**, **governance** (AI Office/Board/Panel), **notified bodies**, and **penalties (Art. 99)** — except Art. 101. National competent authorities designated. *(In force now.)* |
| +24 months | **2 August 2026** | **General application** — the rest of the Act, including **high-risk systems listed in Annex III** (the use-case list) and the **transparency obligations (Art. 50)**. |
| +36 months | **2 August 2027** | **Art. 6(1)** and obligations for **high-risk AI that is a safety component of, or itself, a product regulated under Annex I** (e.g. medical devices, machinery, toys). Also the compliance deadline for GPAI models already on the market before 2 Aug 2025 (Art. 111(3)). |

Additional fixed deadlines: certain public-authority legacy high-risk systems →
**2 August 2030**; large-scale EU IT systems in Annex X → **31 December 2030**.

**Mental model:** prohibitions first (2025), then GPAI + governance (Aug 2025),
then the high-risk core (Aug 2026), then high-risk *embedded in regulated
products* (Aug 2027).

## 3. CURRENT STATUS — the "Digital Omnibus" (as of 22 June 2026)

> ⚠️ **The high-risk timeline is being postponed, but the postponement is NOT
> yet law.** Until it is published in the Official Journal, the dates in §2
> remain legally binding. Present the new dates as *pending*, never as settled.

**What it is.** The European Commission's *Digital Omnibus on AI* — a
simplification package that performs a "stop-the-clock" on the AI Act's high-risk
and several other deadlines, and adds one new prohibition.

**Where it stands (procedural history):**

- **19 November 2025** — Commission tabled the proposal.
- **18 March 2026** — European Parliament committees adopted their position.
- **6 May 2026** — provisional political (trilogue) agreement; confirmed by
  Member-State representatives (Coreper) on 13 May 2026.
- **16 June 2026** — **European Parliament approved the package in plenary.**
- **~29 June 2026 (expected)** — Council formal adoption, then OJ publication;
  the amendments enter into force a few days after publication.

**Legal status today (22 June 2026):** *Agreed by Parliament, awaiting final
Council adoption and Official Journal publication — **not yet in force**.* The
**original 2 August 2026 high-risk date is still the binding law.**

**Postponed / changed dates under the Omnibus (PENDING adoption):**

| Item | Enacted date | Pending new date |
| --- | --- | --- |
| High-risk **Annex III** (standalone use-cases) | 2 Aug 2026 | **2 December 2027** |
| High-risk **Annex I** (regulated/embedded products) | 2 Aug 2027 | **2 August 2028** |
| Synthetic-content marking / watermarking (Art. 50(2)) for systems already on the market | 2 Aug 2026 | **2 December 2026** |
| National regulatory sandboxes | 2 Aug 2026 | **2 August 2027** |

> Source note: a minority of trackers reported the watermarking date as 2 Nov
> 2026 (an earlier committee text); the post-trilogue figure is **2 December
> 2026**. Flag the discrepancy if it matters and check the OJ once published.

**New substantive change (not just a delay):** the Omnibus **adds an Article 5
prohibition** on AI systems generating **non-consensual intimate imagery /
CSAM** ("nudifier" apps), with a safe harbour for systems that have effective
safeguards and a transitional period to ~2 December 2026.

## 4. How an audit should handle the moving timeline

1. **Default to the enacted dates** in §2 as the binding baseline (high-risk
   Annex III = **2 Aug 2026**).
2. **Always surface** the pending Omnibus dates so the user can plan, labelled
   "*approved by Parliament 16 June 2026, pending final adoption — not yet in
   force*."
3. If today's date is after ~early July 2026, **re-verify** whether the Omnibus
   has been published in the OJ. If it has, the pending dates in §3 become the
   operative ones — update the assessment accordingly and cite the OJ.
4. Whatever the high-risk date, the **prohibitions (Art. 5)** and **GPAI
   obligations (Ch. V)** are **already applicable** and are not postponed by the
   Omnibus (except the new NCII/CSAM prohibition's transitional period).
