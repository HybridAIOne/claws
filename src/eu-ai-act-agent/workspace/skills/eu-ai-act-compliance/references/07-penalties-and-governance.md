# Penalties, Enforcement & Governance

*Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. Compiled 22 June 2026. Not legal advice.*

## 1. Administrative fines (Article 99)

For undertakings, the fine is the **higher** of a fixed amount or a percentage of
**total worldwide annual turnover** for the preceding financial year:

| Tier | Trigger | Maximum |
| --- | --- | --- |
| (i) | **Prohibited practices** (Art. 5) | **€35,000,000 or 7%** |
| (ii) | Non-compliance with **other obligations** — providers, deployers, importers, distributors, notified bodies, **and transparency (Art. 50)** | **€15,000,000 or 3%** |
| (iii) | Supplying **incorrect / incomplete / misleading information** to authorities or notified bodies | **€7,500,000 or 1%** |

- **SME / start-up rule (Art. 99(6)):** the fine is capped at the **lower** of
  the two figures (the reverse of the default) — so SMEs face the smaller amount.
- Member States set the national penalty rules (effective, proportionate,
  dissuasive); **specific national ceilings and procedures vary** and several
  Member States were still legislating these in 2026.

## 2. Fines for GPAI providers (Article 101)

The **Commission** (not national authorities) may fine GPAI model providers up to
**€15,000,000 or 3%** of worldwide annual turnover for infringing Chapter V,
failing to supply information, or refusing model access for evaluation.

## 3. Enforcement authorities

- **European AI Office** (inside the Commission) — sole supervisor/enforcer for
  **GPAI models**; develops codes of practice and guidelines.
- **National market surveillance authorities** — enforce the rest (prohibited
  practices, high-risk, transparency) and levy Art. 99 fines.
- **European AI Board** — coordinates national authorities for consistent
  application. **Scientific Panel** of independent experts — flags systemic risks
  from GPAI. **Advisory Forum** — stakeholder input.

## 4. Official guidance published so far (cite these, they are persuasive)

| Document | Date | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Guidelines on **prohibited practices** (Art. 5) | 4 Feb 2025 | Published, non-binding |
| Guidelines on the **definition of an AI system** (Art. 3(1)) | 6 Feb 2025 | Published, non-binding |
| **GPAI Code of Practice** (Arts. 53 & 55) | 10 Jul 2025 | Final, voluntary |
| **Guidelines for providers of GPAI models** | 18 Jul 2025 | Published |
| **Draft guidelines on high-risk classification** (Art. 6 / Annex III) | 19 May 2026 | **Draft — in consultation, not final** |
| **Code of Practice on marking AI-generated content** (Art. 50) | mid-2026 | Final, voluntary |

Guidelines are **non-binding** — the Court of Justice of the EU has the final say
on interpretation — but authorities and courts give them significant weight.

## 5. Why this matters to an audit

Fine exposure scales with the **risk tier**: a confirmed Art. 5 hit is the 7%
band; high-risk and transparency failures are the 3% band. When you report
findings, **tie each gap to its tier and its maximum exposure** so the user can
prioritise — and remind them that fines are a backstop; market-surveillance
authorities can also order **withdrawal or recall** of a non-conforming system.
