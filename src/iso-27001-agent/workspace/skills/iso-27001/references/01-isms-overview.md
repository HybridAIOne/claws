# ISMS Overview — Clauses 4–10

*ISO/IEC 27001:2022. Compiled 22 June 2026. Paraphrased, not the standard text.
Not legal/audit advice.*

ISO/IEC 27001 specifies the requirements for an **Information Security Management
System (ISMS)** — a risk-based management system for protecting the
confidentiality, integrity and availability (CIA) of information. An
organisation is **certified against clauses 4–10**; **Annex A** is a reference
set of controls (see [`03-annex-a-controls.md`](03-annex-a-controls.md)), not an
independently certifiable checklist.

## The management-system clauses (Annex SL "Harmonized Structure")

Clauses 1–3 are scope, references and terms. The auditable requirements are:

- **4 — Context of the organisation.** Determine internal/external issues and
  interested parties (and their relevant requirements), define the **ISMS scope
  (4.3)**, and establish the ISMS and its processes (4.4).
- **5 — Leadership.** Top-management commitment, an **information security policy
  (5.2)**, and assigned **roles, responsibilities and authorities (5.3)**.
- **6 — Planning.** Address risks and opportunities; run the **information
  security risk assessment (6.1.2)** and **risk treatment (6.1.3)** — which
  produces the **Statement of Applicability**; set measurable **information
  security objectives (6.2)**; and **plan changes to the ISMS (6.3 — new in
  2022)**.
- **7 — Support.** Resources, **competence (7.2)**, awareness, communication, and
  control of **documented information (7.5)**.
- **8 — Operation.** **Operational planning and control (8.1)**, and carrying out
  the risk assessment **(8.2)** and risk treatment **(8.3)** in practice.
- **9 — Performance evaluation.** **Monitoring/measurement (9.1)**, **internal
  audit (9.2)**, and **management review (9.3)**.
- **10 — Improvement.** **Continual improvement (10.1)** and **nonconformity &
  corrective action (10.2)**. *(Note: clause 10's order is reversed from the 2013
  edition — use 2022 numbering.)*

## PDCA and the risk-based approach

The ISMS runs on **Plan-Do-Check-Act** continual improvement (Plan ≈ 4–7, Do ≈ 8,
Check ≈ 9, Act ≈ 10). The cycle is embedded in the clause logic rather than named
as a heading. Everything is **risk-based**: controls are selected to treat
identified risks, recorded in the Statement of Applicability, and improved over
time — not adopted wholesale.

## What "certified" means

Certification is granted by an **accredited certification body** after a two-stage
audit, then maintained through annual surveillance and three-yearly
recertification (see [`04-certification-and-soa.md`](04-certification-and-soa.md)).
A scanner or this skill **cannot certify** anything; they assess *readiness* and
collect *technical evidence*.

## The ISO/IEC 27000 family (context)

- **ISO/IEC 27000** — overview and **vocabulary** for the ISMS family.
- **ISO/IEC 27002:2022** — **implementation guidance** for the Annex A controls
  (same numbering, no "A." prefix). The companion to Annex A; guidance, not
  certifiable.
- **ISO/IEC 27005** — information security **risk management** guidance.
- **ISO/IEC 27017 / 27018** — **cloud** security and **cloud PII** codes of
  practice.
- **ISO/IEC 27701** — **privacy (PIMS)** extension to 27001/27002.
- **ISO/IEC 27006-1 / 17021-1** — requirements for the bodies that **audit and
  certify** ISMSs (accreditation context).
