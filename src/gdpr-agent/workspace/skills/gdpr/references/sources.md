# Authoritative sources

Ground answers in primary sources, especially where exact wording, current
adequacy/DPF status, or a specific guideline matters. Prefer the host's web
fetch/search tools over guessing; the reference files in this skill are a working
map, not a substitute for the official text.

## Primary law

- **GDPR full text (EUR-Lex)** — Regulation (EU) 2016/679, consolidated:
  <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj>
- **Article-by-article (with recitals), convenient** — <https://gdpr-info.eu/>
- **German BDSG (gesetze-im-internet.de)** — <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/bdsg_2018/>
- **TTDSG / TDDDG (renamed Dec 2024)** — <https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/ttdsg/>
  (cookies/device storage: §25)

## Regulators & guidance

- **EDPB** (European Data Protection Board) — guidelines, recommendations,
  opinions (consent, DPIA, breach, transfers/Schrems II supplementary measures):
  <https://www.edpb.europa.eu/>
- **European Commission — international transfers, adequacy & SCCs**:
  <https://commission.europa.eu/law/law-topic/data-protection_en>
- **EU–US Data Privacy Framework list** (verify a US recipient's certification):
  <https://www.dataprivacyframework.gov/>
- **Germany — BfDI** (federal authority): <https://www.bfdi.bund.de/> · the
  **DSK** (conference of German state authorities) for joint guidance:
  <https://www.datenschutzkonferenz-online.de/>
- Each EU member state has its own supervisory authority; the competent one usually
  follows the **place of establishment** (in Germany, the relevant Bundesland's
  authority for the private sector).

## Key case law to know

- **Schrems II** (CJEU C-311/18, 2020) — invalidated Privacy Shield; SCCs valid but
  require a transfer impact assessment + supplementary measures.
- **Planet49** (CJEU C-673/17, 2019) — pre-ticked boxes are not valid consent;
  cookie consent must be an active opt-in.

## Caveats

- Adequacy decisions, the DPF list, SCC modules, and authority guidance **change** —
  always check the live source before relying on a transfer mechanism or an
  adequacy claim.
- These pointers are stable as of the GDPR/DSGVO regime in 2024–2025; confirm
  anything time-sensitive against the source.
